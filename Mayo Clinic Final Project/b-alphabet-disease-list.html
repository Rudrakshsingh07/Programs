Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with B

Bacterial pneumonia
A type of pneumonia caused by bacterial infection. Symptoms include cough, fever, difficulty breathing, and chest pain.
Bronchitis
An inflammation of the lining of the bronchial tubes, often due to infection. Symptoms include cough, chest discomfort, and difficulty breathing.
Brucellosis
A bacterial infection transmitted from animals to humans, typically through consumption of contaminated food products. Symptoms include fever, muscle pain, and fatigue.
Bursitis
An inflammation of the bursae, small fluid-filled sacs that cushion the joints. Symptoms include pain, swelling, and limited range of motion in the affected joint.
Bipolar disorder
A mental health condition characterized by extreme mood swings that include emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression).
Botulism
A rare but serious illness caused by toxins produced by Clostridium botulinum bacteria. Symptoms include muscle weakness, double vision, and difficulty speaking or swallowing.
Bulimia nervosa
An eating disorder characterized by recurrent episodes of binge eating followed by purging behaviors such as vomiting or excessive exercise.
Balanitis
An inflammation of the glans penis (head of the penis) in males. Symptoms include redness, swelling, and pain.
Bell's palsy
A condition characterized by sudden, temporary weakness or paralysis of the muscles on one side of the face, often resulting in drooping.
Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH)
An enlargement of the prostate gland that can cause urinary symptoms such as frequent or urgent need to urinate, weak urine stream, or difficulty starting urination.
Beriberi
A nutritional deficiency disease caused by a lack of thiamine (vitamin B1). Symptoms include weakness, fatigue, nerve damage, and heart problems.
Biliary colic
Pain caused by gallstones obstructing the bile ducts. Symptoms include severe abdominal pain, often after eating fatty foods.
Blastomycosis
A fungal infection caused by inhaling spores of the fungus Blastomyces dermatitidis. Symptoms include fever, cough, and fatigue.
Black fever (Visceral leishmaniasis)
A parasitic disease transmitted by sandflies. Symptoms include prolonged fever, weight loss, enlarged spleen and liver, and anemia.
Babesiosis
A tick-borne illness caused by parasites of the genus Babesia. Symptoms include fever, chills, sweats, fatigue, and muscle aches.
Beta thalassemia
A genetic blood disorder characterized by reduced production of hemoglobin, leading to anemia and related complications.
Behcet's disease
A rare autoimmune disorder characterized by inflammation of blood vessels throughout the body. Symptoms include mouth sores, genital sores, and eye inflammation.
Barrett's esophagus
A condition in which the lining of the esophagus is damaged by stomach acid, often due to gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD). It can increase the risk of esophageal cancer.
Bartter syndrome
A group of rare kidney disorders characterized by abnormalities in electrolyte balance, leading to symptoms such as dehydration, muscle weakness, and growth delays in children.
Blepharitis
An inflammation of the eyelids, typically at the base of the eyelashes. Symptoms include redness, itching, and flaking of the eyelids.
Bronchiolitis
An inflammation of the small airways in the lungs, usually caused by viral infection. It is most common in infants and young children, and symptoms include cough, wheezing, and difficulty breathing.
Brugada syndrome
A rare genetic heart rhythm disorder that can cause sudden, life-threatening cardiac arrest. Symptoms may include fainting, seizures, or sudden death.
Babinski reflex
A reflex in which the big toe extends and the other toes fan out when the sole of the foot is stimulated. It is a sign of neurological dysfunction when present in adults.
Biliary atresia
A rare liver disorder in infants in which the bile ducts inside and outside the liver are scarred and blocked. It can lead to liver damage and failure if not treated promptly.
