Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases starting with E

Ebola Virus Disease
A severe and often fatal illness in humans caused by the Ebola virus. Symptoms include fever, severe headache, muscle pain, weakness, fatigue, diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and unexplained bleeding or bruising.
Eczema
A chronic skin condition characterized by inflamed, red, dry, and itchy skin. Symptoms include itching, redness, swelling, and sometimes blistering or oozing of the skin.
Ehrlichiosis
An infectious disease transmitted by ticks that causes flu-like symptoms such as fever, headache, fatigue, muscle aches, and sometimes rash.
Epilepsy
A neurological disorder characterized by recurrent seizures, which can vary from brief lapses of attention or muscle jerks to severe and prolonged convulsions.
Erectile Dysfunction
A condition in which a man is unable to achieve or maintain an erection sufficient for sexual intercourse. Symptoms include difficulty getting an erection, difficulty maintaining an erection, and reduced sexual desire.
Endometriosis
A disorder in which tissue that normally lines the inside of the uterus grows outside the uterus. Symptoms include pelvic pain, painful periods, pain during intercourse, and infertility.
Encephalitis
Inflammation of the brain, usually caused by a viral infection. Symptoms include fever, headache, confusion, seizures, and sometimes coma.
Esophageal Cancer
Cancer that develops in the esophagus, the muscular tube that connects the throat to the stomach. Symptoms include difficulty swallowing, chest pain, weight loss, and coughing up blood.
Essential Tremor
A neurological disorder characterized by uncontrollable shaking or trembling, usually in the hands. Symptoms typically worsen with movement or stress.
Erythema Multiforme
A skin condition characterized by target-like lesions or patches on the skin. It is often triggered by infections, medications, or other factors.
Exocrine Pancreatic Insufficiency (EPI)
A condition in which the pancreas does not produce enough digestive enzymes, leading to malabsorption of nutrients. Symptoms include diarrhea, weight loss, abdominal pain, and bloating.
Emphysema
A type of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) characterized by damage to the air sacs in the lungs, leading to difficulty breathing. Symptoms include shortness of breath, coughing, wheezing, and chest tightness.
Enterovirus Infection
An infection caused by enteroviruses, which are a group of viruses that commonly cause mild to severe respiratory and gastrointestinal illness. Symptoms vary depending on the specific virus but may include fever, respiratory symptoms, rash, and diarrhea.
Epidermolysis Bullosa
A group of rare genetic conditions characterized by fragile skin that blisters and tears easily. Symptoms include blistering of the skin, open sores or wounds, scarring, and deformities of the nails and teeth.
Eisenmenger Syndrome
A complication of certain congenital heart defects that causes increased blood flow to the lungs, leading to pulmonary hypertension and eventually reversal of blood flow in the heart. Symptoms include cyanosis (blue or purple skin discoloration), shortness of breath, fatigue, and swelling of the ankles or abdomen.
Erythema Nodosum
A skin condition characterized by painful, red, tender nodules or lumps that develop under the skin, usually on the shins. It is often associated with underlying conditions such as infections, autoimmune disorders, or medications.
Eosinophilic Esophagitis
An allergic inflammatory condition of the esophagus characterized by high levels of eosinophils (a type of white blood cell) in the esophageal tissue. Symptoms include difficulty swallowing, chest pain, food impaction, and heartburn.
Encephalopathy
A broad term used to describe brain dysfunction or damage. It can have various causes, including infections, toxins, metabolic disorders, and brain injury. Symptoms vary but may include confusion, memory loss, seizures, and changes in behavior or consciousness.
Ebstein's Anomaly
A rare congenital heart defect in which the tricuspid valve is abnormally formed and positioned, leading to abnormal blood flow within the heart. Symptoms vary but may include cyanosis, shortness of breath, fatigue, and heart palpitations.
Enthesitis-related Arthritis
A type of juvenile idiopathic arthritis (JIA) characterized by inflammation of the entheses, which are the sites where tendons and ligaments attach to the bones. Symptoms include joint pain, stiffness, swelling, and tenderness, especially in the lower extremities.
Erythropoietic Protoporphyria
A rare genetic disorder characterized by an abnormal accumulation of protoporphyrin in the blood, which makes the skin extremely sensitive to sunlight. Symptoms include painful burning or itching of the skin, redness, swelling, and blistering after sun exposure.
Ewing Sarcoma
A rare type of cancer that primarily affects the bones or soft tissues. It usually occurs in children and young adults. Symptoms depend on the location of the tumor but may include pain, swelling, and a lump or mass.
Epiglottitis
An inflammation of the epiglottis, a flap of tissue at the base of the tongue that covers the windpipe during swallowing to prevent food from entering the airway. It is usually caused by a bacterial infection and can lead to severe respiratory obstruction. Symptoms include sore throat, difficulty swallowing, drooling, and difficulty breathing.
Ectopic Pregnancy
A pregnancy that occurs outside the uterus, usually in the fallopian tube. It is a potentially life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical attention. Symptoms include abdominal pain, vaginal bleeding, and shoulder pain.
