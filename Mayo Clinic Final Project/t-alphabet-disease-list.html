Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with T

Tuberculosis
A bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs, causing coughing, chest pain, and bloody sputum.
Tetanus
A bacterial infection that leads to muscle stiffness and spasms, often starting in the jaw (lockjaw).
Thyroid Disorders
Conditions affecting the thyroid gland, including hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid) and hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid), leading to various symptoms such as weight changes, fatigue, and mood swings.
Tonsillitis
Inflammation of the tonsils, causing sore throat, difficulty swallowing, and fever.
Trichomoniasis
A sexually transmitted infection caused by a parasite, resulting in vaginal discharge, genital itching, and discomfort during urination.
Tourette Syndrome
A neurological disorder characterized by repetitive, involuntary movements and vocalizations called tics.
Testicular Cancer
Cancer that develops in the testicles, often presenting as a painless lump, swelling, or discomfort in the testicular area.
Thalassemia
A genetic blood disorder that affects the production of hemoglobin, leading to anemia, fatigue, and other complications.
Tinea Versicolor
A fungal skin infection that causes patches of discolored skin, usually on the chest, back, and shoulders.
Temporal Arteritis
Also known as giant cell arteritis, it is a condition characterized by inflammation of the arteries, particularly those in the temples, leading to headaches, jaw pain, and vision problems.
Trigeminal Neuralgia
A chronic pain condition affecting the trigeminal nerve, causing sudden, severe facial pain, often triggered by simple actions like chewing or talking.
Transient Ischemic Attack (TIA)
Often referred to as a mini-stroke, TIA is a temporary disruption of blood flow to the brain, resulting in stroke-like symptoms such as weakness, numbness, or difficulty speaking that typically resolve within 24 hours.
Tubular Breast Cancer
A subtype of breast cancer that starts in the milk ducts and grows in a linear or tubular pattern, often presenting as a small, firm lump in the breast.
Tetralogy of Fallot
A congenital heart defect characterized by four structural abnormalities in the heart, leading to symptoms such as cyanosis (bluish skin), shortness of breath, and poor growth in infants.
Tropical Sprue
A gastrointestinal disorder characterized by chronic diarrhea, malabsorption of nutrients, and deficiencies in vitamins and minerals, often seen in tropical regions.
Takayasu's Arteritis
A rare type of vasculitis that primarily affects the large arteries, causing inflammation, narrowing, and potentially leading to symptoms such as fatigue, muscle pain, and vision changes.
Toxoplasmosis
An infection caused by the Toxoplasma gondii parasite, often contracted from contaminated food or cat feces, leading to flu-like symptoms and potentially severe complications in pregnant women and individuals with weakened immune systems.
Tay-Sachs Disease
A rare genetic disorder characterized by the absence of a vital enzyme, leading to the progressive destruction of nerve cells in the brain and spinal cord, resulting in developmental regression, seizures, and eventually death.
Transient Global Amnesia
A sudden, temporary loss of memory function, often characterized by the inability to form new memories or recall recent events, with the individual retaining their identity and cognitive abilities.
Trench Mouth (Acute Necrotizing Ulcerative Gingivitis)
An infection of the gums caused by bacteria, often associated with poor oral hygiene, leading to severe gum pain, bleeding, and foul breath.
Thrombocytopenia
A condition characterized by a low platelet count in the blood, leading to an increased risk of bleeding and bruising.
Tularemia
A rare infectious disease caused by the bacterium Francisella tularensis, often transmitted through contact with infected animals or insect bites, leading to flu-like symptoms, swollen lymph nodes, and potentially severe complications if untreated.
Transverse Myelitis
An inflammation of the spinal cord, leading to symptoms such as weakness, numbness, and loss of bladder or bowel control.
Trachoma
An infectious eye disease caused by the bacterium Chlamydia trachomatis, leading to inflammation of the inner eyelid, scarring, and potentially blindness if left untreated.
Tubulointerstitial Nephritis
An inflammation of the kidney tubules and surrounding tissue, often caused by medications, infections, or autoimmune disorders, leading to symptoms such as kidney dysfunction, blood in the urine, and fluid retention.
Temporal Lobe Epilepsy
A type of epilepsy characterized by recurrent seizures originating from the temporal lobes of the brain, often accompanied by altered consciousness, memory disturbances, and unusual sensations or emotions.
Tinea Capitis
A fungal infection of the scalp, causing itching, redness, and scaling, often leading to hair loss and the formation of patches of baldness.
Transposition of the Great Arteries
A congenital heart defect where the positions of the pulmonary artery and the aorta are switched, leading to abnormal blood flow and potentially life-threatening complications in newborns.
Thoracic Outlet Syndrome
A group of disorders resulting from compression of the nerves or blood vessels in the thoracic outlet area, leading to symptoms such as pain, numbness, and weakness in the neck, shoulder, and arm.
Tumor Lysis Syndrome
A potentially life-threatening complication that can occur as a result of cancer treatment, where the rapid breakdown of cancer cells releases large amounts of potassium, phosphorus, and uric acid into the bloodstream, leading to electrolyte imbalances and kidney failure.
Transmissible Spongiform Encephalopathy
A group of rare neurodegenerative diseases caused by abnormal proteins called prions, leading to progressive brain damage, cognitive decline, and eventually death.
Tropical Pulmonary Eosinophilia
A rare complication of parasitic infections such as filariasis, characterized by inflammation and excess eosinophils in the lungs, leading to symptoms such as cough, wheezing, and difficulty breathing.
