Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with V

Vitamin D Deficiency
A condition caused by insufficient levels of vitamin D in the body, leading to symptoms such as bone pain, muscle weakness, and fatigue.
Varicella (Chickenpox)
A highly contagious viral infection characterized by an itchy rash consisting of small, red bumps that eventually develop into fluid-filled blisters.
Vasculitis
Inflammation of the blood vessels, which can lead to a variety of symptoms depending on which vessels are affected, including fever, fatigue, weight loss, and organ damage.
Vertigo
A sensation of spinning or dizziness, often due to inner ear problems, neurological issues, or certain medications.
Viral Hepatitis
Inflammation of the liver caused by a viral infection, with symptoms ranging from mild flu-like symptoms to jaundice, abdominal pain, and liver failure.
Ventricular Fibrillation
A life-threatening cardiac arrhythmia characterized by rapid, erratic contractions of the heart's ventricles, leading to loss of consciousness and sudden cardiac arrest.
Vitiligo
A skin condition characterized by the loss of skin color in patches, resulting from the destruction of melanocytes, the cells responsible for producing pigment.
Von Willebrand Disease
A genetic bleeding disorder caused by deficient or defective von Willebrand factor, a protein that helps blood to clot properly, leading to prolonged bleeding and easy bruising.
Varicose Veins
Enlarged, swollen, and twisted veins that usually appear blue or dark purple, often occurring in the legs and causing discomfort, pain, and aching.
Vulvovaginitis
Inflammation or infection of the vulva and vagina, typically causing symptoms such as vaginal itching, burning, abnormal discharge, and discomfort during urination or intercourse.
Valley Fever
An infectious disease caused by inhaling spores of the fungus Coccidioides, leading to flu-like symptoms such as fever, cough, chest pain, and fatigue.
Viral Pneumonia
Pneumonia caused by a viral infection, resulting in inflammation of the lungs and symptoms such as cough, fever, shortness of breath, and fatigue.
Victorian Influenza (La Grippe)
Historical term referring to the severe influenza pandemic of 1899-1900, caused by the H3N8 influenza virus, which spread rapidly across the globe, leading to high mortality rates.
Vulvar Cancer
Cancer that develops in the vulva, the external female genital organs, often presenting as a lump, ulcer, or abnormal growth on the vulva, with symptoms such as itching, pain, and bleeding.
Ventricular Tachycardia
A rapid heart rhythm originating from the heart's ventricles, which can lead to symptoms such as palpitations, dizziness, fainting, and cardiac arrest if left untreated.
Vestibular Neuritis
Inflammation of the vestibular nerve, usually caused by a viral infection, resulting in sudden onset of vertigo, nausea, vomiting, and difficulty with balance and coordination.
Vaginal Yeast Infection
An overgrowth of the fungus Candida in the vagina, leading to symptoms such as itching, burning, irritation, and abnormal discharge.
Valvular Heart Disease
Heart disease affecting one or more of the heart valves, causing symptoms such as chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, and palpitations.
Vitamin B12 Deficiency
A condition caused by inadequate levels of vitamin B12 in the body, leading to symptoms such as fatigue, weakness, pale or jaundiced skin, and neurological problems.
Vitamin K Deficiency
A deficiency of vitamin K, a key nutrient necessary for blood clotting, leading to increased risk of bleeding and easy bruising.
Vascular Dementia
A decline in cognitive function caused by impaired blood flow to the brain, resulting in memory loss, confusion, difficulty with language and reasoning, and other symptoms of dementia.
Visceral Leishmaniasis (Kala-Azar)
A serious parasitic disease caused by infection with the protozoa Leishmania, leading to symptoms such as fever, weight loss, enlarged spleen and liver, and anemia.
Variegate Porphyria
A rare genetic disorder characterized by a deficiency of the enzyme responsible for heme production, leading to symptoms such as abdominal pain, skin sensitivity to light, neurological problems, and psychiatric disturbances.
Vector-Borne Diseases
Diseases transmitted to humans through the bites of infected vectors such as mosquitoes, ticks, and fleas, including malaria, dengue fever, Lyme disease, and Zika virus infection.
Visual Impairment
A broad term encompassing various degrees of vision loss or impairment, ranging from partial to total blindness, caused by conditions such as refractive errors, cataracts, glaucoma, and age-related macular degeneration.
Vulvar Vestibulitis Syndrome
A chronic pain disorder characterized by pain and discomfort in the vestibular area of the vulva, often triggered by touch or pressure, leading to dyspareunia (painful intercourse) and decreased sexual function.
Vanishing White Matter Disease
A rare genetic disorder affecting the brain's white matter, leading to neurological symptoms such as developmental regression, movement abnormalities, seizures, and progressive loss of motor and cognitive function.
Vaginal Atrophy
Thinning, drying, and inflammation of the vaginal walls due to decreased estrogen levels, often occurring after menopause, leading to symptoms such as vaginal dryness, itching, burning, and pain during intercourse.
Vogt-Koyanagi-Harada Syndrome
An autoimmune disorder affecting the eyes, skin, and other organs, characterized by bilateral uveitis (inflammation of the uvea), skin pigment changes, hair loss, and neurologic symptoms.
