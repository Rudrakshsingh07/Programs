Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with Letter U

Ulcerative Colitis
A chronic inflammatory bowel disease characterized by inflammation in the digestive tract.
Urticaria (Hives)
Red, itchy welts on the skin that result from a reaction to food, medication, or other allergens.
Umbilical Hernia
A condition in which part of the intestine protrudes through the abdominal wall near the navel.
Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)
An infection in any part of the urinary system, including the kidneys, bladder, ureters, and urethra.
Urinary Incontinence
The involuntary leakage of urine, often due to weakened bladder muscles.
Uveitis
Inflammation of the uvea, the middle layer of the eye that includes the iris, ciliary body, and choroid.
Upper Respiratory Infection (URI)
An infection of the nose, throat, or sinuses, commonly referred to as the common cold.
Ulnar Nerve Entrapment
Compression or irritation of the ulnar nerve, which runs from the neck to the hand, leading to pain, numbness, or weakness in the arm or hand.
Ulcer
An open sore or lesion on the skin or mucous membrane, often accompanied by inflammation.
Unstable Angina
Chest pain or discomfort that occurs when the heart doesn't receive enough oxygen-rich blood, often a precursor to a heart attack.
Usher Syndrome
A genetic disorder characterized by hearing loss and gradual vision loss due to retinitis pigmentosa.
Uterine Fibroids
Noncancerous growths that develop in the uterus, often causing heavy menstrual bleeding, pelvic pain, or frequent urination.
Ulcerative Keratitis
Inflammation of the cornea, often caused by infection, injury, or underlying conditions.
Uremia
A buildup of waste products in the blood due to kidney failure, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and weakness.
Ulnar Collateral Ligament (UCL) Injury
An injury to the ligament on the inner side of the elbow, commonly known as "pitcher's elbow" in athletes.
Undifferentiated Connective Tissue Disease (UCTD)
A condition characterized by symptoms of autoimmune diseases such as lupus, but without meeting the full criteria for any specific autoimmune disorder.
Unipolar Depression
A mood disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and a lack of interest or pleasure in activities.
Ulnar Drift
A deformity of the fingers in which they deviate toward the ulnar side of the hand, commonly seen in rheumatoid arthritis.
Umbilical Granuloma
A small mass of tissue that forms at the site of the umbilical cord after it falls off, often causing discharge or irritation.
Urinary Retention
The inability to empty the bladder completely, leading to discomfort, frequent urination, or urinary tract infections.
Urethral Stricture
A narrowing of the urethra due to scar tissue, leading to difficulty urinating or urinary retention.
Uveal Melanoma
A type of cancer that develops in the cells of the uvea, the middle layer of the eye.
Umbilical Cord Prolapse
A rare but serious complication of childbirth in which the umbilical cord slips through the cervix ahead of the baby, potentially cutting off blood flow to the baby.
Urinary Stones (Urolithiasis)
Hard mineral deposits that form in the urinary tract and can cause severe pain, blood in urine, or blockage of urine flow.
Uterine Prolapse
A condition in which the uterus slips down into or protrudes out of the vagina, often due to weakened pelvic muscles.
Ulnar Neuropathy
Nerve damage or compression of the ulnar nerve, leading to pain, numbness, or weakness in the arm or hand.
Upper Motor Neuron Lesion
Dysfunction of the nerve cells that carry signals from the brain to the spinal cord, often resulting in muscle stiffness, weakness, or spasms.
Usher Syndrome Type 1
The most severe form of Usher syndrome, characterized by profound congenital deafness and retinitis pigmentosa.
Uveomeningoencephalitis
An inflammatory disease that affects the eyes, brain, and meninges, often associated with autoimmune conditions.
Ulnar Artery Thrombosis
A blood clot in the ulnar artery, which can lead to pain, numbness, or weakness in the hand or fingers.
Umbilical Vein Varix
A dilation or swelling of the umbilical vein, often detected during prenatal ultrasound examinations.
