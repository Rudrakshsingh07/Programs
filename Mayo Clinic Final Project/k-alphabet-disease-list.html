Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with K

Kidney Stones
A condition where hard deposits of minerals and salts form in the kidneys, causing severe pain, nausea, and blood in urine.
Kingella kingae Infection
An infection typically affecting children, causing symptoms such as fever, joint pain, swelling, and occasionally skin rash.
Klinefelter Syndrome
A genetic disorder in males characterized by the presence of an extra X chromosome, resulting in infertility, reduced muscle mass, and enlarged breasts.
Knee Osteoarthritis
A degenerative joint disease affecting the knees, causing pain, stiffness, swelling, and decreased range of motion.
Kuru
A rare and fatal neurodegenerative disorder caused by prions, leading to symptoms such as tremors, difficulty walking, and cognitive decline.
Kawasaki Disease
A childhood illness characterized by inflammation of blood vessels, resulting in fever, rash, swollen lymph nodes, and redness in the eyes and mouth.
Kleptomania
A mental health disorder characterized by the inability to resist the urge to steal items, often not needed for personal use or monetary gain.
Knee Bursitis
An inflammation of the bursae (small fluid-filled sacs) around the knee joint, causing pain, swelling, and limited movement.
Keratitis
Inflammation of the cornea, often due to infection, injury, or autoimmune conditions, leading to eye pain, redness, blurred vision, and light sensitivity.
Kidney Cancer
A type of cancer that originates in the kidneys, often presenting symptoms such as blood in urine, abdominal pain, fatigue, and unexplained weight loss.
Klebsiella Pneumonia
An infection of the lungs caused by the bacterium Klebsiella pneumoniae, resulting in symptoms such as fever, cough, chest pain, and difficulty breathing.
Klinefelter's Syndrome
A genetic disorder characterized by the presence of an extra X chromosome in males, leading to infertility, reduced facial and body hair, and gynecomastia (enlarged breasts).
Kidney Failure
A condition in which the kidneys lose their ability to function properly, leading to accumulation of waste products and fluid in the body, causing symptoms such as fatigue, swelling, and decreased urine output.
Kawasaki Syndrome
An acute febrile illness primarily affecting children under the age of five, characterized by fever, rash, swollen lymph nodes, red eyes, and inflammation of the blood vessels.
Kalazar
Also known as visceral leishmaniasis, it is a parasitic disease transmitted by sandflies, causing symptoms such as fever, weight loss, enlargement of the spleen and liver, and anemia.
Kernicterus
A rare but serious condition in newborns characterized by high levels of bilirubin in the blood, leading to yellowing of the skin and eyes (jaundice), and potentially causing brain damage.
Kennel Cough
An infectious respiratory disease in dogs, characterized by a dry, hacking cough, nasal discharge, and occasionally fever.
Kawasaki Disease
An inflammatory disorder primarily affecting children, characterized by fever, rash, swollen lymph nodes, and inflammation of blood vessels throughout the body.
Keratoacanthoma
A rapidly growing skin tumor that typically appears on sun-exposed areas, such as the face, hands, and arms, often resembling a volcano with a central crater.
Klippel-Trenaunay Syndrome
A rare congenital disorder characterized by the abnormal development of blood vessels, soft tissues, and bones, leading to port-wine stains, varicose veins, and limb overgrowth.
Kernicterus
A severe neurological condition resulting from excessive bilirubin levels in the blood, leading to yellow staining of the brain tissue and causing symptoms such as seizures, developmental delays, and intellectual disabilities.
Keratoconjunctivitis Sicca
Also known as dry eye syndrome, it is a chronic condition characterized by inadequate tear production or excessive tear evaporation, leading to symptoms such as eye irritation, redness, and blurred vision.
Keratosis Pilaris
A common skin condition characterized by small, rough bumps on the skin, typically on the upper arms, thighs, buttocks, or cheeks, often resembling goosebumps or chicken skin.
Kyphosis
An abnormal curvature of the spine, causing a hunchback or rounded upper back appearance, often resulting from poor posture, osteoporosis, or congenital spinal abnormalities.
Kaposi's Sarcoma
A type of cancer that develops from the cells that line lymph or blood vessels, often seen in people with weakened immune systems, such as those with HIV/AIDS, presenting as purple or brown lesions on the skin or mucous membranes.
Keratoacanthoma
A rapidly growing skin tumor that often appears as a dome-shaped nodule with a central crater or plug, usually occurring on sun-exposed areas such as the face, neck, or arms.
Klinefelter Syndrome
A genetic disorder characterized by the presence of an extra X chromosome in males, leading to infertility, reduced facial and body hair, and gynecomastia (enlarged breasts).
Kawasaki Disease
An inflammatory disorder primarily affecting children, characterized by fever, rash, swollen lymph nodes, and inflammation of blood vessels throughout the body.
