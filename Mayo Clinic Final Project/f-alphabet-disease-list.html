Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with F

Fibromyalgia
A disorder characterized by widespread musculoskeletal pain, fatigue, and tenderness in localized areas.
Flu
An infectious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses, presenting symptoms such as fever, cough, sore throat, muscle aches, fatigue, and headaches.
Food Poisoning
An illness caused by consuming contaminated food or water, leading to symptoms like nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, and fever.
Fracture
A broken bone, typically caused by trauma or excessive force, resulting in pain, swelling, bruising, and limited mobility.
Fungal Infection
An infection caused by fungi, resulting in symptoms such as itching, redness, inflammation, peeling, or cracking of the skin.
Frostbite
A condition caused by exposure to extreme cold, leading to damage of skin and underlying tissues, presenting symptoms like numbness, tingling, discoloration, and pain.
Functional Dyspepsia
A chronic disorder of the upper digestive tract, characterized by recurring pain or discomfort in the upper abdomen, bloating, belching, and early satiety, without any evident structural abnormality.
Flatulence
Excessive gas accumulation in the digestive system, leading to bloating, abdominal discomfort, belching, and passing of gas.
Flea-borne Typhus
An infectious disease transmitted by fleas, caused by bacteria of the genus Rickettsia, presenting symptoms like fever, headache, muscle pain, and rash.
Foreign Body Ingestion
The swallowing or inhalation of non-food objects or substances, leading to symptoms such as choking, gagging, pain, difficulty swallowing, and respiratory distress.
Fragile X Syndrome
A genetic disorder characterized by intellectual disability, developmental delays, behavioral problems, and certain physical features, caused by the mutation of the FMR1 gene.
Fasciitis
Inflammation of the fascia, the connective tissue surrounding muscles, leading to pain, swelling, and limited mobility in the affected area.
Fibroids
Non-cancerous growths that develop in the uterus, often causing symptoms such as heavy menstrual bleeding, pelvic pain, frequent urination, and back pain.
Fungal Nail Infection
An infection of the nails caused by fungi, resulting in thickened, discolored, brittle, or distorted nails.
Fanconi Anemia
A rare genetic disorder characterized by bone marrow failure, leading to decreased production of all types of blood cells, as well as various physical abnormalities.
Foix-Alajouanine Syndrome
A rare neurological disorder characterized by progressive paralysis and sensory loss due to spinal cord arteriovenous malformations.
Fuchs' Dystrophy
A progressive eye disorder affecting the cornea, leading to vision loss, glare sensitivity, and blurry or distorted vision.
Fifth Disease (Erythema Infectiosum)
A viral illness caused by parvovirus B19, characterized by a distinctive rash on the face that resembles a "slapped cheek," as well as fever, headache, and body aches.
Facial Palsy (Bell's Palsy)
Weakness or paralysis of the facial muscles, typically on one side of the face, caused by inflammation or compression of the facial nerve, resulting in drooping of the mouth, difficulty closing one eye, and inability to smile symmetrically.
Foot-and-Mouth Disease
A highly contagious viral infection common in children, characterized by fever, sore throat, and the development of painful sores in the mouth and on the hands and feet.
Fibrous Dysplasia
A bone disorder characterized by the abnormal growth of fibrous tissue within the bones, leading to pain, deformity, and increased risk of fractures.
Felty Syndrome
A rare complication of rheumatoid arthritis, characterized by the triad of rheumatoid arthritis, an enlarged spleen (splenomegaly), and a low white blood cell count (neutropenia).
Fatty Liver Disease (Non-alcoholic)
A condition characterized by the accumulation of fat in the liver, not due to excessive alcohol consumption, often associated with obesity, insulin resistance, and metabolic syndrome.
Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis (FSGS)
A kidney disorder characterized by scarring (sclerosis) in specific segments of the kidney's filtering units (glomeruli), leading to proteinuria, edema, hypertension, and progressive loss of kidney function.
Fungal Meningitis
An infection of the meninges (the protective membranes covering the brain and spinal cord) caused by fungi, leading to symptoms such as headache, fever, stiff neck, nausea, and vomiting.
Frozen Shoulder (Adhesive Capsulitis)
A condition characterized by stiffness, pain, and limited range of motion in the shoulder joint, often resulting from inflammation and thickening of the shoulder capsule.
