Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

List of Diseases Starting with A

  • Asthma
    A chronic respiratory condition characterized by difficulty breathing, wheezing, and coughing.
    Shortness of breath, chest tightness, coughing, wheezing.
  • Alzheimer's Disease
    A progressive neurodegenerative disease leading to memory loss and cognitive decline.
    Memory loss, confusion, difficulty in problem-solving, mood swings.
  • Anemia
    A condition characterized by a deficiency of red blood cells or hemoglobin in the blood.
    Fatigue, weakness, pale skin, shortness of breath.
  • Arthritis
    Inflammation of one or more joints, causing pain and stiffness.
    Joint pain, stiffness, swelling, decreased range of motion.
  • AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome)
    A chronic, potentially life-threatening condition caused by the HIV virus, which damages the immune system.
    Fever, fatigue, weight loss, recurrent infections.
  • Acne
    A skin condition characterized by pimples, blackheads, and cysts.
    Pimples, blackheads, whiteheads, oily skin.
  • Allergies
    An abnormal immune response to a usually harmless substance.
    Sneezing, runny or stuffy nose, itchy eyes, rash.
  • Appendicitis
    Inflammation of the appendix, often resulting in severe abdominal pain.
    Abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, loss of appetite.
  • Autism
    A developmental disorder that affects communication and behavior.
    Difficulty in social interaction, repetitive behaviors, communication challenges.
  • Anorexia Nervosa
    An eating disorder characterized by an abnormally low body weight, intense fear of gaining weight, and distorted body image.
    Extreme weight loss, refusal to maintain a healthy weight, distorted body image.
  • Arrhythmia
    An irregular heartbeat rhythm.
    Palpitations, dizziness, fainting, shortness of breath.
  • Anxiety Disorders
    A group of mental health disorders characterized by feelings of anxiety and fear.
    Excessive worrying, restlessness, irritability, panic attacks.
  • Aplastic Anemia
    A rare condition in which the bone marrow fails to produce enough blood cells.
    Fatigue, weakness, frequent infections, paleness.
  • Aphasia
    A language disorder that affects a person's ability to communicate.
    Difficulty speaking, understanding speech, and writing.
  • Addison's Disease
    A disorder that occurs when the adrenal glands do not produce enough hormones.
    Fatigue, weight loss, low blood pressure, skin darkening.
  • Agoraphobia
    An anxiety disorder characterized by fear and avoidance of places or situations that might cause panic or embarrassment.
    Fear of being in crowded places, fear of leaving home alone, panic attacks.
  • Achalasia
    A disorder of the esophagus that makes it difficult to swallow food and liquids.
    Difficulty swallowing, regurgitation of food, chest pain.
  • Asthma
    A chronic respiratory condition characterized by difficulty breathing, wheezing, and coughing.
    Shortness of breath, chest tightness, coughing, wheezing.
  • Acromegaly
    A hormonal disorder that results from excessive growth hormone in the body.
    Enlargement of hands and feet, coarsening of facial features, joint pain.
  • Atopic Dermatitis
    A type of eczema characterized by itchy and inflamed skin.
    Itchy, red, and dry skin, rash, thickened skin.
  • Aplasia Cutis Congenita
    A rare condition where a newborn is missing skin on certain areas of the body.
    Missing skin, shallow ulcers or scars, hair loss in the affected area.
  • Acute Kidney Injury
    A sudden and temporary loss of kidney function.
    Decreased urine output, fluid retention, confusion, fatigue.
  • Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS)
    A severe lung condition caused by a variety of direct and indirect injuries to the lungs.
    Severe shortness of breath, rapid breathing, low blood oxygen levels.
  • Actinic Keratosis
    A rough, scaly patch of skin caused by years of sun exposure.
    Rough, scaly patch of skin, sometimes with a raised bump or horn-like growth.
  • Adenomyosis
    A condition where the tissue that normally lines the uterus grows into the muscular wall of the uterus.
    Heavy menstrual bleeding, severe menstrual cramps, pain during intercourse.
  • Amoebiasis
    An infection of the intestines caused by the amoeba Entamoeba histolytica.
    Abdominal pain, diarrhea, bloody stools, fever.
