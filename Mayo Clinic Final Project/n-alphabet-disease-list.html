Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with "N"

Narcolepsy
A chronic sleep disorder characterized by overwhelming daytime drowsiness and sudden attacks of sleep.
Nephritis
Inflammation of the kidneys, typically caused by an infection or autoimmune reaction.
Neuralgia
Intense, stabbing, or burning pain along the course of a nerve, often caused by irritation or damage to the nerve.
Neurofibromatosis
A genetic disorder that causes tumors to form on nerve tissue, leading to various complications.
Neutropenia
A condition characterized by an abnormally low level of neutrophils, a type of white blood cell important for fighting infections.
Night blindness
A condition in which a person has difficulty seeing in low light or darkness, often due to a deficiency in vitamin A.
Nystagmus
Repetitive, uncontrolled eye movements that may result in reduced vision and depth perception.
Nasal polyps
Noncancerous growths that develop in the lining of the nasal passages or sinuses, often causing congestion and a reduced sense of smell.
Necrotizing fasciitis
A serious bacterial infection that destroys tissue under the skin, often referred to as flesh-eating disease.
Nephrolithiasis
The formation of kidney stones, which are hard deposits of minerals and salts that can cause severe pain.
Niemann-Pick disease
A group of rare inherited disorders that involve the abnormal accumulation of lipids in cells, leading to various symptoms depending on the specific type.
Non-Hodgkin lymphoma
A type of cancer that originates in the lymphatic system, involving abnormal growth of white blood cells called lymphocytes.
Necrotizing enterocolitis
A serious condition that primarily affects premature infants, characterized by inflammation and death of tissue in the intestines.
Nephrotic syndrome
A kidney disorder characterized by the presence of excessive protein in the urine, low levels of protein in the blood, and swelling.
Nasopharyngeal cancer
A type of cancer that develops in the nasopharynx, the upper part of the throat behind the nose.
Neonatal jaundice
A condition in newborns characterized by yellowing of the skin and eyes due to elevated levels of bilirubin in the blood.
Nephrogenic diabetes insipidus
A kidney disorder that causes excessive thirst and urination due to the inability of the kidneys to concentrate urine.
Neuroblastoma
A type of cancer that develops in immature nerve cells, usually occurring in the adrenal glands, abdomen, chest, or spinal cord.
Niemann-Pick disease
A group of rare inherited disorders that involve the abnormal accumulation of lipids in cells, leading to various symptoms depending on the specific type.
Neurogenic bladder
A dysfunction of the urinary bladder due to damage to the nerves that control bladder function, leading to problems with urination.
Neurosyphilis
An infection of the central nervous system caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum, which can occur at any stage of syphilis infection.
Niemann-Pick disease
A group of rare inherited disorders that involve the abnormal accumulation of lipids in cells, leading to various symptoms depending on the specific type.
Nocardiosis
An infectious disease caused by bacteria of the genus Nocardia, which typically affects the lungs but can spread to other parts of the body.
Norovirus infection
A highly contagious viral infection that causes gastroenteritis, with symptoms including diarrhea, vomiting, and stomach cramps.
Nasal congestion
Blockage or obstruction of the nasal passages, often resulting in difficulty breathing through the nose.
Neonatal respiratory distress syndrome
A breathing disorder that affects newborns, especially premature infants, due to immature lungs and inadequate surfactant production.
Neurocysticercosis
A parasitic infection of the central nervous system caused by the larval stage of the pork tapeworm, resulting in various neurological symptoms.
Necrotizing pneumonia
A severe form of pneumonia characterized by necrosis of lung tissue, often caused by aggressive bacteria such as Staphylococcus aureus.
Night sweats
Episodes of excessive sweating during sleep, often associated with underlying medical conditions such as infections or hormonal disorders.
Nasopharyngitis
Inflammation of the nasal passages and pharynx, commonly known as the common cold.
