Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with M

  • Malaria

    Definition:
    Malaria is a mosquito-borne infectious disease caused by Plasmodium parasites. It is transmitted through the bite of infected Anopheles mosquitoes.
    Symptoms:
    Fever, chills, sweats, headache, nausea, vomiting, muscle pain.

  • Multiple Sclerosis

    Definition:
    Multiple sclerosis (MS) is a chronic autoimmune disease that affects the central nervous system (brain and spinal cord).
    Symptoms:
    Weakness or numbness in one or more limbs, electric shock sensations with certain neck movements, tremors, lack of coordination.

  • Measles

    Definition:
    Measles is a highly contagious viral infection caused by the measles virus.
    Symptoms:
    Fever, cough, runny nose, red eyes, rash that starts on the face and spreads to the body.

  • Migraine

    Definition:
    Migraine is a neurological condition characterized by recurrent headaches that can be moderate to severe. Migraine attacks may also be accompanied by other symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound.
    Symptoms:
    Throbbing or pulsating pain on one side of the head, sensitivity to light and sound, nausea, vomiting.

  • Myocardial Infarction

    Definition:
    Myocardial infarction (MI), commonly known as a heart attack, occurs when blood flow to a part of the heart is blocked for a long enough time that part of the heart muscle is damaged or dies.
    Symptoms:
    Chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, nausea, lightheadedness, sweating.

  • Mumps

    Definition:
    Mumps is a contagious viral infection that primarily affects the salivary glands, resulting in swelling and pain.
    Symptoms:
    Swelling and pain in one or both parotid glands (located on the sides of the face), fever, headache, muscle aches.

  • Macular Degeneration

    Definition:
    Macular degeneration is an eye disease that causes progressive damage to the macula, a small area in the retina responsible for central vision.
    Symptoms:
    Blurred or distorted vision, dark or empty areas in central vision, difficulty seeing details.

  • Menopause

    Definition:
    Menopause is a natural biological process that marks the end of menstruation and fertility in women.
    Symptoms:
    Irregular periods, hot flashes, night sweats, mood swings, vaginal dryness.

  • Melanoma

    Definition:
    Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that develops from the pigment-producing cells known as melanocytes.
    Symptoms:
    Irregularly shaped moles or spots on the skin, changes in the size, shape, or color of existing moles, itching or bleeding.

  • Meningitis

    Definition:
    Meningitis is an inflammation of the meninges, the protective membranes covering the brain and spinal cord, usually caused by an infection.
    Symptoms:
    Sudden onset of fever, headache, stiff neck, nausea, vomiting, sensitivity to light, confusion.
