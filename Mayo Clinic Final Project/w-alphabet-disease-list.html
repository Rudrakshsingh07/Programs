Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with W

1. Wilson's Disease
Symptoms: Fatigue, jaundice, abdominal pain, neurological symptoms (tremor, difficulty walking), psychiatric symptoms (depression, anxiety).
Definition: Wilson's disease is a rare inherited disorder that causes copper to accumulate in the liver, brain, and other vital organs, leading to various symptoms.
2. Whooping Cough (Pertussis)
Symptoms: Severe coughing fits, followed by a high-pitched intake of breath that sounds like a "whoop," vomiting after coughing fits, exhaustion.
Definition: Pertussis, also known as whooping cough, is a highly contagious bacterial infection of the respiratory tract, characterized by severe coughing fits.
3. Wegener's Granulomatosis
Symptoms: Sinusitis, nosebleeds, cough, shortness of breath, joint pain, skin lesions, eye redness, fever, fatigue.
Definition: Wegener's granulomatosis is a rare disorder that causes inflammation of blood vessels in the nose, sinuses, throat, lungs, and kidneys.
4. West Nile Virus
Symptoms: Fever, headache, body aches, joint pains, vomiting, diarrhea, rash, fatigue, weakness, swollen lymph nodes, stiff neck, disorientation, tremors, seizures, paralysis.
Definition: West Nile virus is a mosquito-borne infection that can cause severe neurological symptoms and, in rare cases, death.
5. Waldenström's Macroglobulinemia
Symptoms: Weakness, fatigue, weight loss, easy bruising, nosebleeds, blurred vision, headaches, dizziness, swollen lymph nodes, enlarged spleen, neurological symptoms.
Definition: Waldenström's macroglobulinemia is a rare type of blood cancer that affects white blood cells called lymphocytes, leading to overproduction of a specific type of protein.
6. West Syndrome
Symptoms: Infantile spasms (jerking of the arms and legs), developmental delays, intellectual disability, behavioral problems, seizures.
Definition: West syndrome, also known as infantile spasms, is a rare type of epilepsy that typically begins in infancy and is characterized by a specific type of seizure called infantile spasms.
7. Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
Symptoms: Frequent infections, easy bruising, abnormal bleeding, eczema, autoimmune disorders, increased risk of cancer.
Definition: Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome is a rare inherited disorder characterized by a weakened immune system and a reduced ability to form blood clots.
8. Whipple's Disease
Symptoms: Abdominal pain, diarrhea, weight loss, malabsorption, joint pain, fever, fatigue, anemia, neurological symptoms.
Definition: Whipple's disease is a rare bacterial infection that primarily affects the small intestine, leading to malabsorption and a range of other symptoms.
9. Wilms Tumor
Symptoms: Abdominal swelling or mass, abdominal pain, blood in the urine, fever, hypertension, weight loss, poor appetite.
Definition: Wilms tumor, also known as nephroblastoma, is a type of kidney cancer that primarily affects children.
10. Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome
Symptoms: Confusion, memory loss, coordination problems, vision changes, hallucinations, mental health issues.
Definition: Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome is a neurological disorder caused by severe deficiency of thiamine (vitamin B1), often due to chronic alcoholism.
11. Windburn
Symptoms: Red, irritated skin; burning sensation; dryness; peeling; itching.
Definition: Windburn is a condition caused by exposure to cold, windy conditions, resulting in irritation and redness of the skin.
12. Whiplash
Symptoms: Neck pain, stiffness, headaches, dizziness, shoulder pain, back pain, tingling or numbness in the arms.
Definition: Whiplash is a neck injury caused by a sudden jerking motion of the head, typically occurring in motor vehicle accidents.
13. Wilson-Mikity Syndrome
Symptoms: Hypotonia (low muscle tone), seizures, respiratory problems, feeding difficulties, developmental delays.
Definition: Wilson-Mikity syndrome is a rare neurological disorder that affects newborns, characterized by seizures and developmental delays.
14. Westphal's Disease
Symptoms: Muscle stiffness, rigidity, tremors, difficulty with movement, speech problems.
Definition: Westphal's disease, also known as Huntington's disease, is a hereditary neurological disorder that causes degeneration of brain cells, leading to movement, cognitive, and psychiatric symptoms.
15. Wallenberg Syndrome
Symptoms: Difficulty swallowing, hoarseness, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, balance problems, facial numbness or pain, difficulty speaking, drooping eyelid, double vision.
Definition: Wallenberg syndrome, also known as lateral medullary syndrome, is a neurological condition caused by damage to the lateral part of the medulla oblongata in the brainstem.
16. Waterhouse-Friderichsen Syndrome
Symptoms: Sudden fever, vomiting, diarrhea, fatigue, headache, muscle aches, rash, bleeding under the skin, low blood pressure, shock.
Definition: Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome is a rare but potentially life-threatening condition characterized by bleeding into the skin and adrenal gland failure, often caused by severe bacterial infections such as meningococcemia.
17. Waldmann Disease
Symptoms: Severe diarrhea, weight loss, malnutrition, weakness, fatigue, abdominal pain, bloating.
Definition: Waldmann disease, also known as primary intestinal lymphangiectasia, is a rare disorder characterized by abnormal dilation of lymph vessels in the small intestine, leading to protein-losing enteropathy and malabsorption.
18. Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome Protein
Symptoms: Recurrent infections, eczema, abnormal bleeding, autoimmune disorders.
Definition: Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome protein (WASP) is a protein that plays a crucial role in the function of certain immune cells. Mutations in the gene encoding WASP lead to Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome, a rare immune disorder.
19. Werner Syndrome
Symptoms: Premature aging, gray hair, thinning and graying of scalp hair, wrinkled skin, cataracts, diabetes, osteoporosis, cancer predisposition.
Definition: Werner syndrome is a rare genetic disorder characterized by premature aging and an increased risk of age-related diseases such as cancer and heart disease.
20. White Piedra
Symptoms: White or light-colored nodules or masses on hair shafts, brittle hair, itching, hair breakage.
Definition: White piedra is a fungal infection of the hair shafts caused by certain species of fungi, leading to the formation of white or light-colored nodules on the hair.
