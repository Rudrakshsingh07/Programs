Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with I

Idiopathic Pulmonary Fibrosis
A chronic and progressive lung disease characterized by scarring of the lung tissue, which leads to breathing difficulties and decreased oxygen levels in the blood.
Influenza (Flu)
An infectious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses. Symptoms include fever, cough, sore throat, body aches, fatigue, and sometimes vomiting and diarrhea.
Insomnia
A sleep disorder characterized by difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep, leading to insufficient or poor-quality sleep. Symptoms include difficulty concentrating, irritability, and daytime fatigue.
Interstitial Cystitis
A chronic bladder condition characterized by pelvic pain, urinary urgency, frequent urination, and discomfort in the bladder area.
Iron-deficiency Anemia
A condition characterized by a lack of sufficient iron in the body, leading to reduced production of red blood cells and decreased oxygen-carrying capacity in the blood. Symptoms include fatigue, weakness, pale skin, shortness of breath, and dizziness.
Ischemic Stroke
A type of stroke that occurs when a blood vessel supplying blood to the brain is obstructed by a clot, leading to a lack of oxygen and nutrients to brain tissue. Symptoms include sudden weakness or numbness in the face, arm, or leg, difficulty speaking, and confusion.
Impetigo
A contagious skin infection caused by bacteria, characterized by red sores or blisters that burst and form a yellowish crust. It commonly affects children.
Irregular Heartbeat (Arrhythmia)
A condition characterized by abnormal electrical activity in the heart, leading to irregular heart rhythms. Symptoms include palpitations, dizziness, fainting, chest pain, and shortness of breath.
Interstitial Lung Disease
A group of lung disorders characterized by inflammation and scarring of the lung tissue, leading to breathing difficulties and decreased lung function. Symptoms include shortness of breath, cough, and fatigue.
Impaired Glucose Tolerance (Pre-diabetes)
A condition in which blood glucose levels are higher than normal but not high enough for a diagnosis of diabetes. It increases the risk of developing type 2 diabetes and cardiovascular disease. Symptoms may not be present initially.
Insulin Resistance
A condition in which cells in the body become resistant to the effects of insulin, leading to high blood sugar levels. It is a precursor to type 2 diabetes. Symptoms may include fatigue, increased hunger, and weight gain.
Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD)
A group of inflammatory conditions of the colon and small intestine, including Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis. Symptoms include abdominal pain, diarrhea, rectal bleeding, weight loss, and fatigue.
Impaired Vision
A condition characterized by reduced visual acuity or clarity, making it difficult to see clearly. It can result from various eye conditions, such as refractive errors, cataracts, glaucoma, or macular degeneration.
Interstitial Nephritis
An inflammation of the kidney's interstitial tissue, often caused by medications, infections, or autoimmune diseases. Symptoms include fever, rash, blood in the urine, and reduced urine output.
Ischemic Heart Disease (Coronary Artery Disease)
A condition characterized by reduced blood flow to the heart muscle due to narrowing or blockage of the coronary arteries. Symptoms include chest pain or discomfort (angina), shortness of breath, fatigue, and weakness.
Impaired Kidney Function
A condition in which the kidneys are unable to effectively filter waste products from the blood, leading to the accumulation of toxins and electrolyte imbalances. Symptoms may include fatigue, swelling, changes in urine output, and nausea.
Inflammatory Arthritis
A group of autoimmune diseases that cause inflammation of the joints, leading to pain, stiffness, swelling, and reduced mobility. Examples include rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, and ankylosing spondylitis.
Invasive Candidiasis
A serious fungal infection caused by Candida species, which can affect various organs in the body, such as the bloodstream, heart, brain, and kidneys. Symptoms depend on the affected organ but may include fever, chills, and organ dysfunction.
Infantile Spasms
A type of seizure disorder that occurs in infants, characterized by brief, sudden, and repetitive muscle contractions or spasms. It can lead to developmental delays and cognitive impairments if not treated early.
Intrauterine Growth Restriction (IUGR)
A condition in which a fetus does not grow at the expected rate during pregnancy, leading to a lower birth weight and potential health problems for the baby. Causes include maternal factors, placental abnormalities, and fetal factors.
Interstitial Keratitis
An inflammation of the cornea's middle layer, often caused by infections, autoimmune diseases, or trauma. Symptoms include eye pain, redness, light sensitivity, and blurred vision.
Iron Overload Disorders (Hemochromatosis)
A group of inherited or acquired conditions characterized by excessive accumulation of iron in the body's tissues and organs. Symptoms may include fatigue, joint pain, abdominal pain, and liver dysfunction.
Infantile Hemangioma
A common type of benign tumor made up of small blood vessels, typically appearing in infants within the first few weeks of life. Most hemangiomas resolve on their own without treatment, but some may require intervention if they cause complications.
Ischemic Colitis
An inflammatory condition of the colon caused by reduced blood flow to the colon, usually due to a blood clot or atherosclerosis of the mesenteric arteries. Symptoms include abdominal pain, bloody diarrhea, and fever.
Insulinoma
A rare tumor of the pancreas that produces excessive amounts of insulin, leading to hypoglycemia (low blood sugar levels). Symptoms include sweating, confusion, palpitations, and hunger, which improve with sugar intake.
Infective Endocarditis
An infection of the inner lining of the heart chambers and valves, typically caused by bacteria or fungi entering the bloodstream and attaching to damaged heart tissue. Symptoms include fever, fatigue, heart murmur, and sometimes, skin lesions.
Intracerebral Hemorrhage
A type of stroke that occurs when a blood vessel ruptures and bleeds into the brain tissue, leading to damage and pressure on surrounding brain structures. Symptoms include sudden severe headache, weakness, numbness, or paralysis on one side of the body, and altered consciousness.
Idiopathic Thrombocytopenic Purpura (ITP)
A bleeding disorder characterized by low platelet counts due to immune-mediated destruction of platelets. Symptoms include easy bruising, petechiae (small purple or red spots on the skin), and prolonged bleeding from minor injuries.
Inguinal Hernia
A condition in which abdominal contents protrude through the inguinal canal, a passage in the lower abdominal wall. It often presents as a bulge in the groin area and may cause discomfort or pain, especially when coughing or lifting heavy objects.
