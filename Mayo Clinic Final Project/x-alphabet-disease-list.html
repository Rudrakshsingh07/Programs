Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with X

Xeroderma pigmentosum
A genetic disorder characterized by extreme sensitivity to ultraviolet (UV) rays from sunlight, resulting in sunburn, skin cancer, and other skin abnormalities.
Xerophthalmia
A condition caused by vitamin A deficiency, leading to dryness of the conjunctiva and cornea of the eyes, night blindness, and ultimately blindness if left untreated.
Xerosis
The medical term for dry skin, often characterized by itching, flaking, and cracking.
X-linked agammaglobulinemia
An inherited disorder characterized by a lack of mature B cells, leading to a weakened immune system and recurrent infections.
Xanthelasma
Yellowish plaques that develop on the eyelids, typically near the inner canthus, often associated with elevated levels of cholesterol and other lipids.
Xanthogranulomatous pyelonephritis
An uncommon, severe form of kidney infection characterized by the formation of yellowish nodules, typically due to chronic urinary tract obstruction and infection.
Xanthoma
Yellowish deposits of cholesterol underneath the skin, often appearing as nodules or plaques, commonly associated with lipid disorders.
Xenophobia
A psychological condition characterized by an irrational fear or hatred of strangers, foreigners, or people from different cultures.
Xerostomia
Commonly known as dry mouth, it is a condition characterized by a lack of saliva production, leading to discomfort, difficulty swallowing, and an increased risk of dental problems.
Xerotic eczema
A type of eczema characterized by extremely dry, itchy, and cracked skin, often exacerbated by environmental factors such as low humidity and harsh soaps.
X-linked hypophosphatemia
A hereditary disorder characterized by low levels of phosphate in the blood, leading to weakened bones, bone pain, skeletal deformities, and an increased risk of fractures.
Xylitol poisoning
A condition caused by ingestion of xylitol, a sugar substitute commonly found in sugar-free gum and other products, leading to low blood sugar, seizures, liver failure, and potentially death in dogs.
Xenotropic murine leukemia virus-related virus (XMRV)
A retrovirus initially thought to be associated with prostate cancer and chronic fatigue syndrome, though subsequent research has questioned its pathogenicity in humans.
Xerosis cutis
Another term for dry skin, characterized by rough, scaly, or cracked skin due to loss of moisture.
Xenon poisoning
A rare condition caused by exposure to excessive levels of xenon gas, leading to symptoms such as dizziness, nausea, vomiting, confusion, and in severe cases, respiratory failure and death.
Xerophytic
Referring to plants adapted to dry environments, characterized by features such as thick waxy coatings, reduced leaf surface area, and specialized root systems for water storage.
Xerophagy
The practice of eating only dry food, often as a religious or ascetic discipline.
Xerogel
A highly porous material with a dry, gel-like consistency, used in various applications including as a desiccant, catalyst support, and in drug delivery systems.
Xanthoderma
A yellow discoloration of the skin, often associated with liver disease, jaundice, or certain medications.
Xenogeneic
Referring to tissues, organs, or cells transplanted between different species, such as from animals to humans.
Xenobiotic
A chemical substance not naturally produced or expected to be found in the body, often referring to drugs, environmental pollutants, or other foreign compounds.
