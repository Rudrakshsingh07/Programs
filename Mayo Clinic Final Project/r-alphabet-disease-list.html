Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with R

Rheumatoid Arthritis
A chronic inflammatory disorder affecting many joints, including those in the hands and feet, causing pain, swelling, stiffness, and eventually joint deformity and bone erosion.
Rosacea
A common skin condition that causes redness and visible blood vessels in your face. It may also produce small, red, pus-filled bumps.
Rickets
A softening and weakening of bones in children, usually because of an extreme and prolonged vitamin D deficiency, leading to skeletal deformities and growth retardation.
Raynaud's Disease
A condition causing some areas of the body — such as fingers and toes — to feel numb and cold in response to cold temperatures or stress.
Ringworm
A fungal infection of the skin characterized by a red, circular rash with clearer skin in the middle. It can cause itching, redness, and scaling.
Rheumatic Fever
An inflammatory disease that can develop as a complication of untreated strep throat or scarlet fever. It affects the heart, joints, skin, and brain.
Rubella
Also known as German measles, it is a contagious viral infection characterized by a distinctive red rash. Symptoms include fever, sore throat, and swollen glands.
Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS)
A neurological disorder characterized by an uncontrollable urge to move the legs, usually due to uncomfortable sensations such as creeping, itching, or pulling.
Reactive Arthritis
An inflammatory type of arthritis that occurs as a reaction to an infection in another part of the body. It typically affects joints, eyes, and urinary tract.
Renal Failure
The condition where the kidneys fail to adequately filter waste products from the blood. It can be acute or chronic, leading to a buildup of toxins in the body.
Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) Infection
A common respiratory virus that causes cold-like symptoms but can be severe, especially in infants and older adults. It can lead to bronchiolitis or pneumonia.
Reye's Syndrome
A rare but serious condition that causes swelling in the liver and brain. It often occurs in children recovering from a viral infection, especially after taking aspirin.
Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever
A bacterial infection transmitted through the bite of an infected tick. Symptoms include fever, headache, rash, and muscle aches.
Retinoblastoma
A rare childhood cancer that develops in the retina of the eye. Symptoms may include a white glow in the eye, eye redness, and vision problems.
Rhabdomyolysis
A serious syndrome resulting from the breakdown of skeletal muscle fibers, releasing their contents into the bloodstream. It can lead to kidney damage and other complications.
Reflux Esophagitis
Inflammation of the esophagus caused by the backward flow of stomach acid into the esophagus. Symptoms include heartburn, chest pain, and difficulty swallowing.
Recurrent Aphthous Stomatitis (Canker Sores)
Painful sores or ulcers that develop on the inside of the mouth, usually on the tongue, cheeks, or soft palate. The cause is often unknown but may be related to immune system problems, stress, or certain foods.
Renal Cell Carcinoma
The most common type of kidney cancer, originating in the lining of the proximal convoluted tubule. Symptoms may include blood in the urine, back pain, and weight loss.
Rheumatic Heart Disease
A condition where damage to the heart valves occurs as a complication of rheumatic fever. It can lead to heart failure, stroke, and other complications.
Reactive Hypoglycemia
A condition characterized by low blood sugar (hypoglycemia) that occurs after eating, usually within a few hours. Symptoms may include hunger, shakiness, sweating, and confusion.
Refractive Errors
Common vision problems caused by the shape of the eye preventing light from focusing directly on the retina. Types include nearsightedness (myopia), farsightedness (hyperopia), and astigmatism.
Recurrent Respiratory Papillomatosis
A rare viral infection that causes wart-like growths in the respiratory tract, usually in the larynx or vocal cords. It can cause voice changes, breathing problems, and recurrent infections.
Reiter's Syndrome
An autoimmune condition that typically affects the joints, eyes, urethra, and skin. It may follow a bacterial infection and is characterized by arthritis, eye inflammation, and urinary symptoms.
Respiratory Failure
A condition where the lungs fail to adequately exchange oxygen and carbon dioxide, leading to dangerously low levels of oxygen in the blood. It can be acute or chronic.
Retinal Detachment
A medical emergency where the retina pulls away from its normal position at the back of the eye. Symptoms include sudden flashes of light, floaters, and a curtain-like shadow over your field of vision.
Reactive Lymphadenopathy
Enlargement of lymph nodes in response to an infection, inflammation, or cancer elsewhere in the body. It can cause swelling and tenderness in the affected lymph nodes.
