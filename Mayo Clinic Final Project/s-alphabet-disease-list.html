Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with S

1. Sepsis
Symptoms: Fever, rapid heart rate, rapid breathing, confusion, and low blood pressure.
Definition: Sepsis is a potentially life-threatening condition caused by the body's response to an infection. The immune system releases chemicals into the bloodstream to fight the infection, which can lead to inflammation throughout the body.
2. Schizophrenia
Symptoms: Hallucinations, delusions, disorganized thinking, lack of motivation, and reduced emotional expression.
Definition: Schizophrenia is a chronic and severe mental disorder that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. It is characterized by distorted thoughts, perceptions, and emotions.
3. Stroke
Symptoms: Sudden numbness or weakness in the face, arm, or leg, confusion, trouble speaking or understanding speech, difficulty walking, dizziness, and severe headache.
Definition: A stroke occurs when the blood supply to part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, depriving brain tissue of oxygen and nutrients. This can cause brain cells to die and lead to permanent brain damage.
4. Sickle Cell Anemia
Symptoms: Fatigue, pain in the chest, arms, legs, and abdomen, jaundice, and frequent infections.
Definition: Sickle cell anemia is a genetic disorder characterized by abnormal hemoglobin in the red blood cells. These abnormal cells can become rigid and sticky, blocking blood flow and causing pain and organ damage.
5. Shingles
Symptoms: Pain, burning, numbness, or tingling followed by a rash that usually appears as a single stripe of blisters on one side of the body.
Definition: Shingles is a viral infection caused by the varicella-zoster virus, the same virus that causes chickenpox. After a person has had chickenpox, the virus can remain dormant in the body and reactivate years later, causing shingles.
6. Strep Throat
Symptoms: Sore throat, fever, swollen tonsils, difficulty swallowing, and red spots on the roof of the mouth.
Definition: Strep throat is a bacterial infection caused by the Streptococcus bacteria. It is characterized by inflammation and pain in the throat, and it is highly contagious.
7. Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)
Symptoms: Fatigue, joint pain and swelling, skin rashes, fever, and sensitivity to light.
Definition: Systemic lupus erythematosus is an autoimmune disease in which the body's immune system mistakenly attacks healthy tissues, leading to inflammation and damage in various parts of the body.
8. Sleep Apnea
Symptoms: Loud snoring, episodes of breathing cessation during sleep, gasping for air during sleep, and daytime sleepiness.
Definition: Sleep apnea is a sleep disorder characterized by pauses in breathing or shallow breaths during sleep. It can lead to poor sleep quality and daytime fatigue.
9. Seasonal Allergies (Allergic Rhinitis)
Symptoms: Sneezing, runny or stuffy nose, itchy or watery eyes, and coughing.
Definition: Seasonal allergies, also known as allergic rhinitis, occur when the immune system overreacts to allergens such as pollen, mold, or pet dander, leading to inflammation and symptoms in the nose and eyes.
10. Sarcoidosis
Symptoms: Fatigue, swollen lymph nodes, shortness of breath, cough, skin rashes, and joint pain.
Definition: Sarcoidosis is a condition characterized by the growth of inflammatory cells in various organs, most commonly the lungs and lymph nodes. The exact cause is unknown.
11. Salmonella Infection
Symptoms: Diarrhea, fever, abdominal cramps, nausea, and vomiting.
Definition: Salmonella infection is a bacterial infection caused by the Salmonella bacteria. It is typically contracted by consuming contaminated food or water.
12. Scoliosis
Symptoms: Uneven shoulders, one shoulder blade that appears more prominent than the other, uneven waist, and leaning to one side.
Definition: Scoliosis is a sideways curvature of the spine that most often occurs during the growth spurt just before puberty. In severe cases, it can cause pain and breathing difficulties.
13. Sjögren's Syndrome
Symptoms: Dry eyes, dry mouth, difficulty swallowing, fatigue, and joint pain.
Definition: Sjögren's syndrome is an autoimmune disorder in which the immune system attacks the glands that produce moisture in the body, leading to dryness in the eyes, mouth, and other areas.
14. Sickle Cell Disease
Symptoms: Pain, anemia, swelling in the hands and feet, frequent infections, and delayed growth.
Definition: Sickle cell disease is a group of inherited red blood cell disorders. It is characterized by abnormal hemoglobin that causes red blood cells to become rigid and sickle-shaped, leading to blockages in blood vessels and other complications.
15. Stevens-Johnson Syndrome
Symptoms: Painful red or purplish rash that spreads and blisters, facial swelling, and tongue swelling.
Definition: Stevens-Johnson syndrome is a rare and serious disorder of the skin and mucous membranes. It is usually a reaction to medication or an infection and requires emergency medical treatment.
16. Swine Flu (H1N1 Influenza)
Symptoms: Fever, cough, sore throat, runny or stuffy nose, body aches, headache, chills, and fatigue.
Definition: Swine flu, caused by the H1N1 influenza virus, is a respiratory illness that spreads from person to person, primarily through coughing or sneezing.
17. Syphilis
Symptoms: Sores (chancre) on the genitals, rectum, or mouth, rash, fever, swollen lymph nodes, and hair loss.
Definition: Syphilis is a sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum. It progresses through stages if left untreated and can cause serious health complications.
18. Systemic Sclerosis (Scleroderma)
Symptoms: Thickening and tightening of the skin, Raynaud's phenomenon, joint pain, muscle weakness, and digestive problems.
Definition: Systemic sclerosis, also known as scleroderma, is a chronic autoimmune disease characterized by the hardening and tightening of the skin and connective tissues. It can affect internal organs as well.
19. Scarlet Fever
Symptoms: Red rash that feels like sandpaper, sore throat, fever, headache, and swollen glands.
Definition: Scarlet fever is a bacterial infection caused by group A Streptococcus bacteria. It primarily affects children and is characterized by a rash and fever.
20. Spondylitis
Symptoms: Back pain, stiffness, and reduced range of motion in the spine.
Definition: Spondylitis is a type of arthritis that primarily affects the spine. It causes inflammation and stiffness in the joints between the vertebrae.
21. Skin Cancer
Symptoms: Changes in the skin, such as a new growth, a sore that doesn't heal, or a change in an existing mole.
Definition: Skin cancer is the abnormal growth of skin cells, most often caused by exposure to ultraviolet radiation from the sun or tanning beds.
22. Smallpox
Symptoms: Fever, fatigue, headache, and rash with pus-filled blisters.
Definition: Smallpox is a contagious and sometimes fatal disease caused by the variola virus. It was declared eradicated in 1980 after a successful vaccination campaign.
23. Stomach Ulcers (Peptic Ulcers)
Symptoms: Burning stomach pain, nausea, vomiting, bloating, and weight loss.
Definition: Stomach ulcers, also known as peptic ulcers, are open sores that develop on the lining of the stomach, small intestine, or esophagus. They are often caused by infection with Helicobacter pylori bacteria or long-term use of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).
24. Swimmer's Ear (Otitis Externa)
Symptoms: Ear pain, itching, redness, and drainage of pus.
Definition: Swimmer's ear, or otitis externa, is an infection of the outer ear canal, typically caused by bacteria or fungi. It is often associated with swimming or exposure to water.
25. Sprains
Symptoms: Pain, swelling, bruising, and limited range of motion in the affected joint.
Definition: A sprain is an injury to a ligament, the tough, fibrous tissue that connects bones to each other at a joint. Sprains occur when a joint is forced beyond its normal range of motion, causing stretching or tearing of the ligament.
26. Sinusitis
Symptoms: Facial pain or pressure, nasal congestion, nasal discharge, cough, and reduced sense of smell.
Definition: Sinusitis is inflammation or swelling of the tissue lining the sinuses. It can be caused by infection, allergies, or other factors and may be acute or chronic.
