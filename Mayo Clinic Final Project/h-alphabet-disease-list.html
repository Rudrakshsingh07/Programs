Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with H

  • Hepatitis

    Definition:
    Hepatitis is an inflammation of the liver, most commonly caused by a viral infection.
    Symptoms:
    Fatigue, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, jaundice.

  • Hypertension

    Definition:
    Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a long-term medical condition in which the blood pressure in the arteries is persistently elevated.
    Symptoms:
    Often asymptomatic, but may include headaches, shortness of breath, and nosebleeds.

  • Heart Disease

    Definition:
    Heart disease refers to several types of heart conditions, including coronary artery disease, heart rhythm problems (arrhythmias), and heart defects present at birth (congenital heart defects).
    Symptoms:
    Chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, fatigue, irregular heartbeats.

  • HIV/AIDS

    Definition:
    HIV (human immunodeficiency virus) weakens the immune system by destroying important cells that fight disease and infection. AIDS (acquired immunodeficiency syndrome) is the most advanced stage of HIV infection.
    Symptoms:
    Fever, fatigue, swollen lymph nodes, weight loss, night sweats.

  • Hemorrhoids

    Definition:
    Hemorrhoids are swollen veins in the lowest part of your rectum and anus. Sometimes the walls of these blood vessels stretch so thin that the veins bulge and get irritated, especially when you poop.
    Symptoms:
    Bleeding during bowel movements, itching or irritation in the anal region, pain or discomfort.

  • Hypothyroidism

    Definition:
    Hypothyroidism is a condition in which your thyroid gland doesn't produce enough of certain crucial hormones.
    Symptoms:
    Fatigue, weight gain, constipation, dry skin, depression.

  • Huntington's Disease

    Definition:
    Huntington's disease is a progressive brain disorder that causes uncontrolled movements, emotional problems, and loss of thinking ability (cognition).
    Symptoms:
    Chorea (involuntary jerking or writhing movements), changes in personality, difficulty with motor skills.

  • Hyperthyroidism

    Definition:
    Hyperthyroidism is a condition in which your thyroid gland produces too much of the hormone thyroxine.
    Symptoms:
    Weight loss, rapid heartbeat, sweating, nervousness, irritability.

  • Hypoglycemia

    Definition:
    Hypoglycemia is a condition characterized by an abnormally low level of blood sugar (glucose), usually below 70 mg/dl.
    Symptoms:
    Shakiness, dizziness, sweating, hunger, irritability.

  • Hemophilia

    Definition:
    Hemophilia is a rare disorder in which your blood doesn't clot normally because it lacks sufficient blood-clotting proteins (clotting factors).
    Symptoms:
    Excessive bleeding and easy bruising, prolonged bleeding from cuts or injuries, joint pain and swelling.

  • Hidradenitis Suppurativa

    Definition:
    Hidradenitis suppurativa is a chronic skin condition characterized by clusters of abscesses or subcutaneous boil-like "nodules" that develop in areas of the body where skin rubs together, such as the armpits, groin, buttocks, and under the breasts.
    Symptoms:
    Painful lumps under the skin, pus-filled lesions, scarring.

  • Hand, Foot, and Mouth Disease

    Definition:
    Hand, foot, and mouth disease is a highly contagious viral infection that's common in young children. It causes sores in the mouth and a rash on the hands and feet.
    Symptoms:
    Fever, sore throat, blisters on hands, feet, and inside the mouth.

  • Herpes

    Definition:
    Herpes is a viral infection caused by the herpes simplex virus (HSV). There are two types of herpes: HSV-1 (oral herpes) and HSV-2 (genital herpes).
    Symptoms:
    Painful sores, blisters, itching, burning, flu-like symptoms.

  • Hypercholesterolemia

    Definition:
    Hypercholesterolemia is a condition characterized by high levels of cholesterol in the blood. It's often caused by unhealthy lifestyle choices, such as poor diet and lack of exercise.
    Symptoms:
    Often asymptomatic, but can lead to cardiovascular diseases such as heart attacks and strokes.

  • Hypochondria

    Definition:
    Hypochondria, also known as illness anxiety disorder, is a mental health condition characterized by excessive worry about having a serious illness.
    Symptoms:
    Preoccupation with having a serious illness despite medical reassurance, frequent doctor visits, excessive health-related internet searches.

  • Hyperhidrosis

    Definition:
    Hyperhidrosis is a medical condition characterized by excessive sweating beyond what is necessary to regulate body temperature.
    Symptoms:
    Excessive sweating, particularly in the palms, soles, armpits, or face.

  • Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy

    Definition:
    Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) is a disease in which the heart muscle (myocardium) becomes abnormally thick (hypertrophied).
    Symptoms:
    Shortness of breath, chest pain, palpitations, dizziness, fainting.

  • Hemochromatosis

    Definition:
    Hemochromatosis is a genetic disorder in which the body absorbs too much iron from the diet. The excess iron is stored in the body's tissues and organs, where it can cause damage.
    Symptoms:
    Fatigue, joint pain, abdominal pain, liver problems.

  • Hashimoto's Disease

    Definition:
    Hashimoto's disease is an autoimmune disorder that causes inflammation of the thyroid gland, leading to hypothyroidism.
    Symptoms:
    Fatigue, weight gain, sensitivity to cold, dry skin, constipation.

  • Hypoparathyroidism

    Definition:
    Hypoparathyroidism is a rare condition in which the parathyroid glands fail to produce sufficient parathyroid hormone (PTH).
    Symptoms:
    Muscle cramps, tingling or burning sensation in the hands and feet, fatigue, dry skin, hair loss.

  • Hypothermia

    Definition:
    Hypothermia occurs when your body loses heat faster than it can produce heat, causing a dangerously low body temperature.
    Symptoms:
    Shivering, confusion, slurred speech, drowsiness, shallow breathing.

  • Hyperemesis Gravidarum

    Definition:
    Hyperemesis gravidarum is a severe form of nausea and vomiting during pregnancy that can lead to dehydration, weight loss, and electrolyte imbalances.
    Symptoms:
    Persistent nausea and vomiting, dehydration, weight loss.

  • Hypoxia

    Definition:
    Hypoxia is a condition in which the body or a region of the body is deprived of adequate oxygen supply at the tissue level.
    Symptoms:
    Shortness of breath, rapid breathing, confusion, blue skin or lips.

  • Hyponatremia

    Definition:
    Hyponatremia is a condition characterized by low levels of sodium in the blood.
    Symptoms:
    Nausea, headache, confusion, seizures, coma.

  • Hepatorenal Syndrome

    Definition:
    Hepatorenal syndrome is a type of kidney failure that occurs as a complication of severe liver disease.
    Symptoms:
    Decreased urine output, fluid retention, confusion, jaundice.
