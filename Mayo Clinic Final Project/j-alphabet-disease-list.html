Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with J

Juvenile Arthritis
A form of arthritis that affects individuals under the age of 16. Symptoms include joint pain, stiffness, and swelling.
Jaundice
A condition characterized by yellowing of the skin and eyes due to high levels of bilirubin in the blood. Symptoms also include fatigue, abdominal pain, and dark urine.
Juvenile Diabetes (Type 1 Diabetes)
A chronic condition where the pancreas produces little to no insulin. Symptoms include increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, and weight loss.
Jock Itch
A fungal infection of the skin in the groin area. Symptoms include itching, redness, and a rash.
Johne's Disease
A chronic bacterial infection that primarily affects the intestines of cattle, sheep, and goats. Symptoms include weight loss, diarrhea, and decreased milk production.
Japanese Encephalitis
A viral infection transmitted by mosquitoes, leading to inflammation of the brain. Symptoms include fever, headache, neck stiffness, disorientation, and seizures.
Junctional Epidermolysis Bullosa
A rare genetic disorder characterized by fragile skin that blisters and tears easily. Symptoms include blistering of the skin, scarring, and deformities of the nails and teeth.
Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)
A chronic autoimmune disease in children characterized by joint inflammation. Symptoms include joint pain, swelling, stiffness, and fever.
Jet Lag
A temporary sleep disorder that occurs when a person's internal body clock is out of sync with the time zone they are in. Symptoms include fatigue, insomnia, irritability, and difficulty concentrating.
Jakob-Creutzfeldt Disease (CJD)
A rare, degenerative, and fatal brain disorder caused by abnormal prion proteins. Symptoms include rapidly progressive dementia, muscle stiffness, involuntary movements, and eventually coma.
Jellyfish Sting
An injury caused by the venomous tentacles of jellyfish. Symptoms include intense pain, redness, swelling, itching, and in severe cases, difficulty breathing and cardiac arrest.
Juvenile Myoclonic Epilepsy
A type of epilepsy characterized by brief, involuntary muscle jerks or twitches, particularly upon waking. Symptoms also include absence seizures and generalized tonic-clonic seizures.
Juvenile Huntington's Disease
A rare form of Huntington's disease that manifests in childhood or adolescence. Symptoms include movement abnormalities, cognitive decline, and psychiatric disturbances.
Juvenile Nasopharyngeal Angiofibroma (JNA)
A benign tumor that typically arises in the nasopharynx of adolescent males. Symptoms include nasal congestion, epistaxis (nosebleeds), facial swelling, and visual disturbances.
Juvenile Rheumatoid Arthritis (JRA)
An autoimmune disease affecting children under the age of 16, characterized by joint inflammation. Symptoms include joint pain, swelling, stiffness, and fever.
Jacobsen Syndrome
A rare chromosomal disorder caused by deletion of genetic material from chromosome 11. Symptoms vary widely but may include intellectual disability, distinctive facial features, heart defects, and developmental delays.
Job Syndrome (Hyper-IgE Syndrome)
A rare genetic disorder characterized by recurrent infections, eczema, and elevated levels of immunoglobulin E (IgE). Symptoms also include skin abscesses, recurrent pneumonia, and skeletal abnormalities.
Juvenile Spondyloarthritis
A type of arthritis that affects children and adolescents, primarily involving inflammation of the spine and joints. Symptoms include back pain, stiffness, and reduced range of motion.
Juvenile Myelomonocytic Leukemia (JMML)
A rare form of leukemia that primarily affects young children. Symptoms include anemia, easy bruising or bleeding, frequent infections, and swollen lymph nodes.
Juvenile Polyposis Syndrome
A rare genetic disorder characterized by the growth of multiple polyps in the gastrointestinal tract. Symptoms include rectal bleeding, abdominal pain, diarrhea, and anemia.
Juvenile Pilocytic Astrocytoma
A type of brain tumor that typically occurs in children and adolescents. Symptoms vary depending on the location of the tumor but may include headaches, nausea, vomiting, and seizures.
Jones Fracture
A fracture of the fifth metatarsal bone in the foot. Symptoms include pain, swelling, bruising, and difficulty bearing weight on the affected foot.
Jervell and Lange-Nielsen Syndrome (JLNS)
A rare genetic disorder characterized by a prolonged QT interval on electrocardiogram (ECG), leading to an increased risk of life-threatening cardiac arrhythmias. Symptoms include fainting, seizures, and sudden cardiac arrest.
Junctional Rhythm
An abnormal heart rhythm originating in the atrioventricular (AV) junction. Symptoms may include palpitations, dizziness, lightheadedness, and syncope (fainting).
Japanese B Encephalitis
A viral infection transmitted by mosquitoes, leading to inflammation of the brain. Symptoms include fever, headache, neck stiffness, disorientation, and seizures.
Juvenile Paget's Disease
A rare genetic disorder characterized by accelerated bone turnover, leading to bone pain, fractures, and deformities. Symptoms may also include hearing loss and neurological complications.
Juvenile Polyarteritis
An autoimmune vasculitis affecting children and adolescents, involving inflammation of small and medium-sized blood vessels. Symptoms include fever, fatigue, weight loss, joint pain, and skin rash.
Junctional Ectopic Tachycardia
An abnormal heart rhythm characterized by rapid heartbeats originating in the atrioventricular (AV) junction. Symptoms may include palpitations, chest pain, dizziness, and fainting.
Jalili Syndrome
A rare genetic disorder characterized by cone-rod dystrophy (a type of vision impairment) and amelogenesis imperfecta (abnormal development of tooth enamel). Symptoms also include nystagmus and photophobia.
Juvenile Temporal Arteritis
An inflammatory disease affecting the temporal arteries in children and adolescents. Symptoms include headache, scalp tenderness, jaw pain, and vision problems.
