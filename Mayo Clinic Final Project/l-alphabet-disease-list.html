Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with "L"

Lupus
A chronic autoimmune disease characterized by inflammation of various parts of the body, particularly the skin, joints, kidneys, and brain.
Lyme Disease
An infectious disease caused by bacteria transmitted through the bite of infected black-legged ticks. Symptoms include fever, headache, fatigue, and a characteristic skin rash called erythema migrans.
Leukemia
A type of cancer that affects the blood and bone marrow, characterized by the overproduction of abnormal white blood cells. Symptoms include fatigue, easy bruising, and susceptibility to infections.
Liver Cirrhosis
A late stage of scarring (fibrosis) of the liver caused by many forms of liver diseases and conditions, such as hepatitis and chronic alcoholism. Symptoms include fatigue, jaundice, and abdominal pain.
Lung Cancer
A malignant tumor that starts in the cells of the lung. Symptoms may include coughing, chest pain, shortness of breath, and weight loss.
Lymphoma
A type of cancer that affects the lymphatic system, including the lymph nodes, spleen, and other organs of the immune system. Symptoms may include swollen lymph nodes, fever, and weight loss.
Laryngitis
Inflammation of the larynx (voice box) due to overuse, infection, or irritation. Symptoms include hoarseness, sore throat, and difficulty speaking.
Leishmaniasis
A parasitic disease caused by the Leishmania parasite, transmitted through the bite of infected sandflies. Symptoms vary depending on the type of leishmaniasis but may include skin sores, fever, and enlarged spleen.
Lactose Intolerance
A digestive disorder in which the body is unable to digest lactose, a sugar found in milk and dairy products. Symptoms include bloating, diarrhea, and abdominal cramps.
Lupus Nephritis
Inflammation of the kidneys caused by systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), a chronic autoimmune disease. Symptoms may include blood in the urine, swelling of the legs, and high blood pressure.
Laryngeal Cancer
Cancer that develops in the tissues of the larynx (voice box). Symptoms may include hoarseness, difficulty swallowing, and a persistent sore throat.
Lymphedema
Swelling that occurs due to the accumulation of lymph fluid in the tissues, usually in the arms or legs. It can be caused by lymph node removal, injury, or congenital conditions.
Lipoma
A benign tumor made up of fat tissue. Lipomas are usually painless and most often found in the neck, shoulders, back, or arms.
Lead Poisoning
A condition caused by exposure to lead, typically through contaminated water, paint, or dust. Symptoms may include abdominal pain, constipation, and neurological issues.
Labyrinthitis
Inflammation of the labyrinth, a part of the inner ear that helps control balance and hearing. Symptoms include vertigo, nausea, and hearing loss.
Lyme Carditis
A rare complication of Lyme disease where the bacteria infect the heart. Symptoms may include chest pain, palpitations, and shortness of breath.
Liver Abscess
A pus-filled cavity in the liver, usually caused by a bacterial infection. Symptoms may include fever, abdominal pain, and jaundice.
Lewy Body Dementia
A progressive neurological disorder characterized by the presence of abnormal protein deposits (Lewy bodies) in the brain. Symptoms may include cognitive decline, visual hallucinations, and movement problems.
Lymphangitis
Inflammation of the lymphatic vessels, usually due to an infection. Symptoms include red streaks on the skin, swelling, and pain along the affected vessels.
Legionnaires' Disease
A severe form of pneumonia caused by the Legionella bacteria. Symptoms include fever, chills, cough, and muscle aches.
Liver Cancer
Cancer that starts in the liver cells. Symptoms may include abdominal pain, unexplained weight loss, and jaundice.
Lichen Planus
An inflammatory skin condition characterized by itchy, flat-topped bumps that may be shiny and reddish-purple in color. It can affect the skin, mouth, nails, and genitals.
Laryngopharyngeal Reflux (LPR)
A condition where stomach acid backs up into the throat, leading to irritation and inflammation. Symptoms may include hoarseness, chronic cough, and throat clearing.
Lymphocytic Colitis
A type of inflammatory bowel disease characterized by chronic inflammation of the colon. Symptoms include watery diarrhea, abdominal pain, and weight loss.
Langerhans Cell Histiocytosis
A rare disorder characterized by an overproduction of Langerhans cells, a type of immune cell. It can affect various organs and systems in the body, leading to a wide range of symptoms.
Lymphoproliferative Disorders
A group of disorders characterized by the abnormal growth of lymphocytes (a type of white blood cell). These disorders include lymphomas and leukemias.
Liposarcoma
A type of cancer that develops in the fat cells. Liposarcomas can occur anywhere in the body but are most commonly found in the limbs or abdomen.
