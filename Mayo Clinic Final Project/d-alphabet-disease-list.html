Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with D

Dengue Fever
A mosquito-borne viral infection causing fever, headache, joint and muscle pains, and a characteristic skin rash.
Diabetes
A metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar levels over a prolonged period, leading to symptoms such as increased thirst, frequent urination, and unexplained weight loss.
Depression
A mood disorder causing persistent feelings of sadness and loss of interest in activities, along with various physical and cognitive symptoms.
Dementia
A broad category of brain diseases that cause long-term and often gradual decrease in the ability to think and remember, affecting daily functioning.
Dermatitis
An inflammation of the skin, often resulting in itching, redness, and swelling, with various possible causes including allergic reactions, irritants, or genetic factors.
Diphtheria
An infectious disease caused by the bacterium Corynebacterium diphtheriae, characterized by a sore throat, fever, and the formation of a thick membrane in the throat and nose.
Down Syndrome
A genetic disorder caused by the presence of all or part of a third copy of chromosome 21, leading to developmental delays, intellectual disability, and distinctive physical features.
Diverticulitis
An inflammation or infection of small pouches called diverticula that develop along the walls of the intestines, causing abdominal pain, fever, and changes in bowel habits.
Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)
A blood clot that forms in a deep vein, usually in the legs, causing swelling, pain, warmth, and redness in the affected area, with potential for serious complications if the clot breaks loose and travels to the lungs.
Dyslexia
A learning disorder characterized by difficulty reading, spelling, and processing language, often despite normal intelligence and adequate education.
Drug Addiction
A chronic relapsing disorder characterized by compulsive drug seeking, use, and cravings despite harmful consequences, often accompanied by physical dependence and withdrawal symptoms.
Delirium
An acute and fluctuating disturbance in attention, awareness, and cognition, often occurring in the context of an underlying medical condition or substance intoxication.
Diverticulosis
A condition characterized by the presence of diverticula, or small bulges, in the walls of the digestive tract, usually without symptoms but may cause abdominal pain, bloating, and changes in bowel habits.
Dry Eye Syndrome
A chronic condition characterized by insufficient production of tears or poor quality of tears, leading to symptoms such as dryness, irritation, and blurred vision.
Dental Caries
Also known as tooth decay or cavities, dental caries is a common infectious disease characterized by demineralization of tooth enamel, leading to the formation of cavities and potential tooth loss.
Dysentery
An infectious disease of the intestines, typically caused by bacteria, parasites, or viruses, and characterized by severe diarrhea with blood or mucus, abdominal pain, fever, and dehydration.
Dystonia
A movement disorder characterized by involuntary muscle contractions that cause repetitive or twisting movements and abnormal postures, affecting one or more parts of the body.
Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
A genetic disorder characterized by progressive muscle degeneration and weakness, typically starting in childhood and leading to difficulties in walking, breathing, and heart function.
Dehydration
A condition resulting from excessive loss of body fluids, often caused by insufficient intake of fluids, excessive sweating, vomiting, or diarrhea, leading to symptoms such as thirst, dry mouth, fatigue, and dizziness.
Diabetic Retinopathy
A complication of diabetes that affects the eyes, causing damage to the blood vessels in the retina, leading to vision problems and potential blindness if left untreated.
Diabetic Neuropathy
A type of nerve damage that occurs in people with diabetes, causing symptoms such as pain, tingling, numbness, and weakness, usually in the hands and feet.
Dyspepsia
Also known as indigestion, dyspepsia is a common digestive problem characterized by discomfort or pain in the upper abdomen, often accompanied by bloating, belching, and nausea.
Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (DHF)
A severe form of dengue fever characterized by bleeding, low platelet count, and plasma leakage, which can lead to shock, organ failure, and death if not treated promptly.
Dilated Cardiomyopathy
A condition characterized by the enlargement and weakening of the heart muscle, leading to decreased ability to pump blood effectively and potential complications such as heart failure, arrhythmias, and sudden cardiac death.
Depressive Disorder
A mental health disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest or pleasure in activities, often accompanied by changes in sleep, appetite, energy level, and concentration.
Dysfunctional Uterine Bleeding (DUB)
Abnormal uterine bleeding that occurs outside of normal menstruation patterns, often due to hormonal imbalances, uterine fibroids, polyps, or other underlying conditions, leading to unpredictable or excessive bleeding.
Dysthymia
A chronic mood disorder characterized by long-term, low-grade depressive symptoms that persist for at least two years, interfering with daily functioning and overall quality of life.
