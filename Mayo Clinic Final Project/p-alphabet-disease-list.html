Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases Starting with P

Pneumonia

A respiratory infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs. Symptoms include cough, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing.

Polio

A highly infectious viral disease that primarily affects young children. Symptoms can range from fever and headache to paralysis.

Parkinson's Disease

A progressive nervous system disorder that affects movement. Symptoms include tremors, stiffness, and difficulty with balance and coordination.

Psoriasis

A chronic autoimmune condition that causes rapid skin cell growth, resulting in red, scaly patches of skin. Symptoms may include itching, burning, and soreness.

Pancreatitis

Inflammation of the pancreas that can be acute or chronic. Symptoms include severe abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and fever.

Peptic Ulcer Disease

A sore that develops on the lining of the stomach, small intestine, or esophagus. Symptoms include abdominal pain, bloating, and heartburn.

Prostate Cancer

Cancer that develops in the prostate gland in men. Symptoms may include difficulty urinating, blood in the urine, and erectile dysfunction.

Pulmonary Embolism

A blockage in one of the pulmonary arteries in the lungs, usually caused by a blood clot. Symptoms include shortness of breath, chest pain, and coughing up blood.

Pulmonary Hypertension

A type of high blood pressure that affects the arteries in the lungs and the right side of the heart. Symptoms include shortness of breath, fatigue, and chest pain.

Pancreatic Cancer

Cancer that develops in the tissues of the pancreas. Symptoms may include abdominal or back pain, weight loss, and jaundice.

Progeria

A rare genetic condition that causes rapid aging in children. Symptoms include growth failure, loss of body fat and hair, and a characteristic facial appearance.

Porphyria

A group of rare genetic disorders that affect the body's ability to produce heme, a crucial component of hemoglobin. Symptoms vary but may include abdominal pain, skin sensitivity to sunlight, and neurological symptoms.

Pulmonary Fibrosis

A progressive lung disease characterized by scarring of the lung tissue. Symptoms include shortness of breath, dry cough, and fatigue.

Polycystic Kidney Disease

A genetic disorder characterized by the growth of numerous cysts in the kidneys. Symptoms include abdominal pain, high blood pressure, and blood in the urine.

Pleurisy

Inflammation of the pleura, the membranes surrounding the lungs. Symptoms include sharp chest pain, cough, and difficulty breathing.

Preeclampsia

A pregnancy complication characterized by high blood pressure and signs of damage to other organ systems, often the kidneys. Symptoms include swelling, headaches, and vision changes.

Periodontal Disease

A severe gum infection that can damage the soft tissue and bone supporting the teeth. Symptoms include swollen, tender gums, loose teeth, and persistent bad breath.

Polycythemia Vera

A rare blood disorder characterized by the overproduction of red blood cells by the bone marrow. Symptoms include headache, dizziness, and blurred vision.

Prostatitis

Inflammation of the prostate gland, often caused by bacterial infection. Symptoms include pain or burning during urination, frequent urination, and pelvic pain.

Peripheral Neuropathy

A condition that affects the nerves outside of the brain and spinal cord. Symptoms include numbness, tingling, and weakness, often in the hands and feet.

Pulmonary Edema

Buildup of fluid in the air sacs of the lungs, leading to difficulty breathing. Symptoms include extreme shortness of breath, coughing up frothy pink mucus, and chest pain.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

A mental health condition triggered by a terrifying event, either experiencing it or witnessing it. Symptoms may include flashbacks, nightmares, and severe anxiety.

Pernicious Anemia

An autoimmune condition in which the body lacks enough healthy red blood cells due to a deficiency of vitamin B12. Symptoms include fatigue, weakness, and pale or yellowish skin.

Pseudotumor Cerebri

A condition characterized by increased pressure inside the skull that mimics the symptoms of a brain tumor. Symptoms include headache, vision problems, and ringing in the ears.

Pituitary Tumor

A growth or abnormality in the pituitary gland, which can cause hormone imbalances. Symptoms depend on the size and location of the tumor but may include headaches, vision problems, and hormonal changes.
